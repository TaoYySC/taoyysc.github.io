I am Tao Yang, a master's student in Bioinformatics at Tongji University, Shanghai, China. I work in the [Zhang Lab](https://zhanglab.tongji.edu.cn/), School of Life Sciences and Technology, under the joint supervision of **Prof. Menghuan Zhang** and **Prof. Yong Zhang**. I received my B.S. degree in Computer Science and Technology from Shanghai Ocean University in 2024.

My research focuses on **functional phosphorylation site prediction** and **protein localization**, with an emphasis on developing **deep learning** methods for post-translational modification (PTM) analysis and transcription factor (TF) regulatory mechanisms. I am broadly interested in applying machine learning and computational approaches to biological and biomedical problems.

## Current Work

I am developing **[PhosLoc-Transport](https://github.com/TaoYySC/phosloc-transport)**, a direction-aware framework that links transcription factor phosphosites to localization and transcriptional output. The project uses a two-stage pipeline:

1. **Localization-Regulatory Classifier** — integrates local sequence, central phosphosite, and AlphaFold-derived structural features to identify candidate localization-regulatory phosphosites.
2. **Localization Direction Classifier** — predicts whether candidate sites are associated with nuclear accumulation or cytoplasmic redistribution.

The framework is validated with CPTAC tumor multi-omics data to evaluate predicted nuclear accumulation-associated phosphosites against target-gene regulation. More details are available on the [Projects](/portfolio/) page and in the [GitHub repository](https://github.com/TaoYySC/phosloc-transport).
